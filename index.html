MON · STRENGTHSlow sumo squats + Floor hip thrust
Squat: 3s descent, explosive up · Hip thrust: 2s hold at top
×4
  • Sumo squat: feet wide, toes out 45°. Descend slowly in 3 seconds, back straight, chest up. Explode back up.
  • Hip thrust: back against a sofa/bench, feet flat. Drive hips up, squeeze glutes hard for 2 seconds at the top. Lower with control.
  • No rest between squat and hip thrust — perform them as a superset.
💡 Feel it in your glutes? Good. If you feel it in your knees, widen your stance.
MON · STRENGTHReverse lunges + Donkey kickbacks
Back knee 2cm from floor · 12 reps/side
×3
  • Reverse lunge: step one foot back, lower the back knee to ~2cm from the floor. Front thigh parallel to the ground. Push through the front heel to come back up.
  • Donkey kickback: on all fours, kick one leg straight back and up. Squeeze the glute at the top for 1 second. Don't arch your back.
  • Do all reps on one side, then switch. 12 reps each side per set.
💡 Kickback too easy? Add a resistance band just above the knees.
MON · STRENGTHSingle-leg glute bridge + Clamshell
15 reps bridge / 20 reps clamshell per side
×3
  • Single-leg bridge: lie on your back, one knee bent, other leg extended. Drive hip up until body is straight. Squeeze glute at top. 15 reps each side.
  • Clamshell: lie on your side, knees bent at 90°. Keep feet together, open the top knee like a clamshell. 20 reps each side. Targets the outer glutes (gluteus medius).
  • Move straight from bridge to clamshell on the same side, then switch.
💡 The clamshell is one of the most underrated glute exercises. Slow and controlled beats fast every time.
THU · HIITTabata circuit: 20s work / 10s rest
8 exercises × 5 rounds = 20 min
×5
  • 1. Jumping jacks — full body warm-up, arms and legs together
  • 2. Mountain climbers — plank position, drive knees to chest alternately at speed
  • 3. Squat jumps — squat down, explode up, land soft
  • 4. High knees — run on the spot, drive knees up to hip height
  • 5. Modified burpees — step back instead of jumping if needed
  • 6. Alternating reverse lunges — controlled, glutes engaged
  • 7. Push-ups — on knees if needed, chest touches floor
  • 8. Plank shoulder taps — tap opposite shoulder, hips still
⏱ 8 exercises × 30s (20s work + 10s rest) = 4 min/round × 5 rounds = 20 min exact. Use a free Tabata timer app.
THU · HIITFinish: plank 1 min + 20 bicycle crunches
Back to back, no rest · core finisher
×2
  • Plank: forearms on floor, body straight from head to heels. Don't let hips sag or rise. Hold 1 minute breathing steadily.
  • Bicycle crunches: on your back, hands behind head. Bring opposite elbow to knee while extending the other leg. 20 slow reps total (10 each side).
💡 Do 2 sets with 30s rest between. This finishes the core work after the cardio burst.
FRI · COREDead bug 10 reps/side + Plank 40s
No rest between · deep transverse ab work
×4
  • Dead bug: lie on your back, arms pointing to ceiling, knees at 90°. Slowly lower your right arm back + left leg down simultaneously — without letting your lower back lift. Return and switch. 10 reps each side.
  • The key: lower back stays glued to the floor at all times. If it lifts, reduce your range of motion.
  • Plank: straight into forearm plank, hold 40 seconds. Breathe.
💡 Dead bug is better than crunches for deep core — it trains the transverse abdominis, which is what flattens the belly long term.
FRI · CORELeg raises + Reverse crunches
15 + 15 reps · lower abs focus
×4
  • Leg raises: lie flat, hands under your lower back. Raise both legs to 90°, then lower slowly without touching the floor. 15 reps. The slower the descent, the harder it is.
  • Reverse crunch: knees bent, lift hips off the floor by curling them toward your chest. The movement is small and controlled — not a swing. 15 reps.
  • 1 min rest between sets.
💡 Both exercises specifically target the lower belly — the hardest area to strengthen. Quality over speed.
FRI · CORESide plank 30s/side + Scissors 20 reps
Obliques + lower belly · 1 min rest
×3
  • Side plank: on one forearm, body in a straight diagonal line. Hold 30 seconds each side. Keep hips lifted — don't let them sag.
  • Scissors: lie on your back, legs raised to 45°. Cross them slowly left over right, then right over left. 20 total crossings, controlled rhythm.
  • 1 min rest between sets.
💡 Side plank trains the obliques — essential for a defined waist. Don't rush the scissors: slower = harder.

5 min

SAT · HIITIntense circuit: Squat jump + Burpee + Jump lunge
45s work / 15s rest · 5 rounds · ~200 kcal
×5
  • Squat jump: squat down, explode up, land softly with bent knees. If knees hurt, replace with regular squat.
  • Burpee: squat, hands to floor, step or jump feet back to plank, push-up optional, jump feet back, stand and jump. Modify by stepping instead of jumping.
  • Jump lunge: from lunge position, jump and switch legs mid-air. Modify with alternating reverse lunges if knees sensitive.
⏱ 3 exercises × (45s + 15s) = 3 min/round × 5 rounds = 15 min + warm-up = ~20 min session total.
SAT · GLUTESFinish: Hip thrust ×20 + Kickback ×15/side
Back to back · finish the session strong
×3
  • Hip thrust: back on sofa/bench, feet flat, hip-width. Drive hips up, squeeze hard at the top. 20 reps. Feel it in your glutes — not your lower back.
  • Kickback: straight into all-fours position. Extend leg back and up, 1s squeeze at top. 15 reps each side without rest.
  • After 3 sets your glutes should be burning. That's perfect — this is what shapes them.
💡 This finisher at the end of a cardio session is highly effective — the glutes are already warm and blood is flowing.

Sleep management — key point
🌙
Vienna days: bedtime 9:30pm
5:50am wake-up = asleep before 10pm minimum. Ideal: 9:30pm. Pack bag and prep meals the night before so mornings aren't rushed.
⏰
Other days: recover the deficit
Mon/Thu/Fri/Sat: take the opportunity to sleep 7h30–8h. Saturday morning, even with a workout, don't start before 8am to protect recovery.
🧠
Sleep & weight loss
Poor sleep raises ghrelin (hunger hormone) and lowers leptin (satiety hormone). Sleep deprivation sabotages nutrition efforts — it's non-negotiable.
Key points
01
Moderate deficit
Aim for −250/−300 kcal/day, no more. A severe deficit causes muscle loss and cravings. Consistency beats intensity.
02
Protein first
Aim for 90–110g/day (~1.8g/kg). Protects muscle mass during weight loss and keeps hunger at bay longer.
03
No forced workouts
Tue/Wed = intentional rest. Training exhausted after a 5:50am start hurts progress. Recovery is part of the programme.
04
Hydration
1.5–2L of water per day. On train days, bring a bottle. Often "hunger" is actually dehydration.
05
Progress the exercises
When an exercise becomes easy: add reps, slow the tempo (slow = harder), or add 1 set. No equipment needed.
06
Patience & consistency
−4 kg safely = 10–14 weeks. No quick fix. Belly and glutes are the last areas to change — stay the course.
